World Wonder

Taj Mahal

An ivory‑white marble mausoleum built in the 17th century by Mughal emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his wife Mumtaz Mahal.

Location: Agra, Uttar Pradesh, India • Coordinates: 27.1750° N, 78.0419° E

About the Taj Mahal

The Taj Mahal is a marble mausoleum complex on the banks of the Yamuna River in Agra, northern India. Construction began in 1631 and finished around 1653. It is widely regarded as a masterpiece of Mughal architecture, combining elements from Islamic, Persian, Ottoman Turkish, and Indian styles.

Five quick facts

  • Built: 1631–1653 by Emperor Shah Jahan.
  • Purpose: Tomb for Mumtaz Mahal and later Shah Jahan himself.
  • Material: Mainly white Makrana marble with inlay work (semi-precious stones).
  • Location: Agra, Uttar Pradesh, India — on the right bank of the Yamuna River.
  • World Heritage: UNESCO World Heritage Site since 1983 and often called a symbol of love.

Architecture highlights

The complex includes the main tomb, a mosque, a guest house, formal gardens, and four minarets. The main dome reaches about 73 meters high and the building shows perfect symmetry and beautiful decorative calligraphy and pietra dura (stone inlay).

Where is it?

Agra, Uttar Pradesh, India

Visitor tips

  • Best time to visit: early morning at sunrise to avoid crowds and see the marble glow.
  • Wear comfortable shoes — there is a lot of walking across gardens and platforms.
  • Respect the site: it is both a monument and a grave — photography rules apply in some areas.

Short history timeline

  1. 1631: Mumtaz Mahal dies in childbirth; Shah Jahan orders the mausoleum.
  2. 1632–1648: Main construction period; thousands of artisans worked on the site.
  3. 1653: Major construction generally completed (gardens and finishes continued).
  4. 1983: Declared a UNESCO World Heritage Site.
